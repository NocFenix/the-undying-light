With a low hum the bright white overhead lighting kicks on, illuminating the sterile cryochamber. Another hum, mechanical this time, resounds through the chamber as the eight cryopods crack their seals and lift open, revealing the nearly naked bodies of the six sleeping crew members. Inside the pods are the crew of USSEV Bronze Pilgrim:
- Captain Gregory Schmidt
- Warrant Officer Robert Pho
- Navigations Officer Yessa Devash
- Chief Engineer Jern Devash
- Engineer Aleena Kole
- Science Officer Paulette Van

The small crew of the ship have only been together for a short time, Weyland-Yutani corporation having pulled them from different assignments to work together on the Bronze Pilgrim, a Science Exploratory Vessel. The mission of the the Bronze Pilgrim is to head out into the dark edge of known space, and beyond, to discover new worlds for Weyland-Yutani colonization efforts. In addition to the search for new worlds, the crew has been told to be vigilant for any xenomorph, alien, lifeforms while investigating these previously unexplored regions.

Slowly sitting up and stretching, you can hear your joints pop and crack. A yawn breaks free from your parched throat as you stretch, fingers brushing the glass of the cryopod door above you. The cold air sends goosebumps raising across your entire, nearly naked body, and your breath puffs visibily in the air before you. After rubbing the crusty residue from the corner of your eyes you twist in the pod to look over at your twin.

You eventually make your way to the Maintenance Deck near the belly of the Bronze Pilgrim. The area is full of old clanging and dripping pipes, some clearly in need of replacement, but for whatever reason have yet to see that kind of attention. Kole strips off her work coat and toss it onto a crate against one wall and wipes some sweat off her forehead.

"Good god, it's like a sauna down here," she says looking around. "Why is the rest of the ship so damn cold?"

You realize that she is right. There seems to be a large amount of steam in the air the further into the small corridors you wind, as you venture closer to the heating elements. "Best guess is that we have a breached pipe somewhere which is what's causing all of this steam," you reply.

The concept seems sounds, after all, this steam had to come from somewhere. The heating on the Bronze Pilgrim was created by pulling heat from the engines and the reactor and circling it through the cooling pipes that ran throughout the ship. As you think about that, you realize that this could be potentially catastrophic; if something has damaged the cooling system to the point where steam is leaking out this badly, the reactor or the engines could be heating up to critical levels.

You pause to think about the reprecussions of moving forward. If there is an issue with the cooling system, which seems likely, it will need to be patched sooner rather than later. However, that could also mean some kind of coolant or reactor leak, which could mean radiation exposure.

Kole pauses a few steps ahead and turns to look back at you, her flaxen hair plastered to her face by her sweat. She pulls a rag out of her pocket and wipes at her face and neck to clear some of it away.
